Note these pictures are to represent proper color. 
Some of these rabbits are not posed the best.
However it may help you identify what you have.

 

All descriptions are from the ARBA Standard of Perfection, 2011-2015 Edition.

 


The body color is to be black, and even over the entire animal. 
Color is to be as rich as possible and extend well down the hairshaft, with a slate blue undercolor. 
Eyes - Brown.

Main body color is to be black with a slate blue undercolor. 
The belly surface color may range from white to pale cream.
The underside of chin and tail is to be pale cream with a slate blue undercolor. 
The tail may or may not show blue undercolor.
 The marking color between the body color and the belly color is to be orange.
 Nostrils and triangle are to be orange.  Eye circles and inside of the ears are to be light orange. 
Chest will have a mixture of black and lighter orange,
which merges into the main body color in front of the shoulders. 

Eyes - Brown.

The main body is to have a sepia saddle extending from the nape of the neck to the tail,
shading to a paler sepia color on the flanks and chest. 
All shadings are to be gradual, avoiding any blotches or streaks.
 Face, outside of ears, front of the foreleg, outside of the hind feet,
and upper side of the tail are to match the saddle color as close as possible. 
Undercolor is to match the surface color as closely as possible. 
They are to have silver-white nostrils, eye circles, jowls, belly, underside of the tail,
on the inside of the ears, triangle, collar, chest, and on the inside of the front and rear legs. 
Chest will have a mixture of sepia and silver, which merges
 into the main body color in front of the shoulder. 
They are to have evenly distributed, silver-white tipped guard hairs on the sides, flanks, and rump. 
The belly undercolor is to be a light sepia.  The tail may or may not show a light sepia color.
Eyes - Brown.
